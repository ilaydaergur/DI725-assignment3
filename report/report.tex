\begin{abstract}
We apply QLoRA to fine-tune PaliGemma-3B on the RISC remote sensing image captioning dataset.
Training only 0.39\% of model parameters, we achieve substantial gains over the pre-trained baseline:
BLEU-4 improves from 0.0062 to 0.0553, METEOR from 0.0897 to 0.2285,
and ROUGE-L from 0.1476 to 0.2675, demonstrating that parameter-efficient fine-tuning
effectively adapts a general-purpose vision-language model to a specialised domain.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}

Large Vision-Language Models (VLMs) such as PaliGemma~\cite{beyer2024paligemma},
which combines the SigLIP vision encoder with the Gemma language decoder,
exhibit strong general-purpose capabilities but underperform on domain-specific tasks
without adaptation.
Full fine-tuning of a 3B-parameter model is prohibitively expensive in memory and compute.
QLoRA~\cite{dettmers2023qlora} addresses this by combining 4-bit quantisation with
low-rank adapter (LoRA) weights, drastically reducing trainable parameters while
preserving task-specific learning capacity.
We apply this approach to remote sensing image captioning and evaluate the result
quantitatively and qualitatively.

\section{Method}

\subsection{Dataset}
We use the RISC dataset~\cite{riscdata} (\texttt{caglarmert/full\_riscm}),
comprising 44,521 satellite images at 224$\times$224 px resolution
with five human-written captions per image.
The dataset is split 95/5 into training and test sets,
yielding 42,294 training image--caption pairs and 2,226 test images.
Evaluation is performed on a 200-sample subset of the test split.

\subsection{Model and QLoRA Setup}

PaliGemma-3B (\texttt{google/paligemma-3b-pt-224}) is loaded in 4-bit NF4
quantisation via BitsAndBytes.
LoRA adapters with rank $r\!=\!8$ are attached to all seven linear projection layers
of the Gemma language decoder
(\texttt{q}, \texttt{k}, \texttt{v}, \texttt{o}, \texttt{gate}, \texttt{up}, \texttt{down\_proj}),
yielding \textbf{11.3M trainable parameters out of 2.93B (0.39\%)}.
The image is processed by the frozen SigLIP encoder; only the language decoder adapters
are updated during training.

Training uses AdamW-8bit, learning rate $2\!\times\!10^{-5}$,
effective batch size 16 (4 per device $\times$ 4 gradient accumulation steps),
cosine warmup over 100 steps, and 1 epoch over the full training set.
The input prompt is \textit{``describe this image.''} and the caption is the generation target.

Table~\ref{tab:results} and Fig.~\ref{fig:metrics} show consistent improvement across all
metrics after fine-tuning.
BLEU-4 increases nearly 9$\times$, while METEOR and ROUGE-L roughly double.
Low absolute BLEU-4 values are expected for single-reference evaluation and are
consistent with reported results on other captioning benchmarks.
The improvement in METEOR and ROUGE-L is particularly meaningful as these metrics
account for synonym overlap and n-gram recall, better reflecting caption quality.

Fig.~\ref{fig:examples} shows qualitative comparisons.
The baseline model produces generic descriptions, whereas the fine-tuned model
generates domain-specific language characteristic of remote sensing imagery
(e.g., identifying runways, vegetation patterns, building arrangements, and road networks).

\section{Conclusion}

QLoRA enables effective domain adaptation of PaliGemma-3B with only 0.39\% trainable
parameters.
Fine-tuning on the full RISC dataset for a single epoch yields substantial quantitative
and qualitative improvements over the pre-trained baseline,
confirming the viability of parameter-efficient fine-tuning for specialised
vision-language applications in remote sensing.
